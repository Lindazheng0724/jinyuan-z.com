About K.


A high school student

The idea of this series is to capture a common day of my friend Krystal, who is a high school student in Shanghai. Being a high school student is very stressful, especially in a metropolitan like Shanghai where everybody has great expectations. This photo series “About Krystal” portrays the issues that high school students face in contemporary society such as loneliness for not having parents’ companionship, emotional instability caused by puberty, and limited time of sleep due to the large amount of school assignments. The series starts with the early morning scene, in which Krystal waits the arrival of the subway, finishes off by exercising to maintain a good body shape. These mundane tasks reflect a social phenomenon among all high school students, as they are the generations who receive attention only for their academics, but neglected otherwise. I hope my images can disclose the story behind this group and raise awareness about teenagers’ mental and physical health.

About Krystal

This is a photo collage of a morning scene in my city (Shanghai). The scene is composed of delicious breakfast, hardworking workers, morning exercise group and Krystal, a high school student who are waiting for the metro to school. The purpose of this photo is to give the audiences a glance of an ordinary day in the city, and introduce the main character of this series. I used candid photography style and fast shutter speed to capture all these moments, and then used Photoshop to cut those images into the shape of the well-known architecture of Oriental Pearl TV Tower. I combined different scenes to show the diversity of the city, from the elderly to children, and the key character in my project, Krystal, is highlighted with color.
As a high school student, she sometimes is being ignored and receives less attention from contemporary society, other than her education.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Just For a Few minutes

In this photograph, I want to propose a very realistic moment for high school students - taking a nap on the public transportation. Intensive exams and school assignments often take away our precious sleeping hours, therefore we can only use fragmented period to catch up on sleep. I placed my camera two rows after Krystal and shot from my eye level, in order to capture her exhausting facial expression. Also, viewers are easier to be drawn into the photo from my eye level as if they are passengers sitting behind her. I did not purposely alter the color of this image because I want to present this scene in the most realistic way since it is very common in our daily lives.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Bicycles

Although nowadays there are various convenient transportations that we can choose from, bicycles are still Krystal’s favorite. She often commutes in bicycles from home to school, and during this period, she likes to indulge in her imagination as she looks around, observes, tries to capture the moment that she have missed in her busy day, and attains inspirations from the old brick walls and the citizens along the way. Thus, in my picture, I overlapped two images of Krystal riding bicycle, which symbolized an around trip between home and school. I lowered my shutter speed to 1/13 to capture two motion blur images. The photo is warm-toned because I want to express the sense of joy and comfort that Krystal experiences on the bicycle, as she finally has a moment to take a deep breath from all the stress.
Photo by: A6400

Sky

Krystal loves to stare at the boundless sky and zone out, and she always pulls out her camera in order to look at the sky in a closer distance, in her words: she wants to be closer to the sky. To her, the moment of looking up can temporarily relief pressures and ease her worries. In this picture, I was standing one step behind Krystal, and lowered my camera into an angle to focus on her cellphone and the blue sky that she was staring. Then I saturated the color of the sky view on her phone screen to portray a “perfect” world that seems to be far away from her.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Friends

For most high school students in Shanghai, they lack companionship since their parents are busy with their work, therefore, despite classmates, pets are the only ones they can spend time with. I used reflection in this photo because pets are somewhat similar to us, as they also desire attention from others. I placed two characters, Krystal and the dog Lamber into mirrored position, and then cut out the opposite two characters and lower their transparency to create contrast and demonstrate the idea that they complement each other.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Jump

This photo is inspired by the well-known photographer Henri Cartier-Bresson’s “The Decisive Moment”. In this image, Krystal is jumping over a puddle from yesterday’s rain. This is an enjoyable moment many of us have when we were little kids, and I want to capture that innocent joy and sense of easiness that are within us. I set shutter speed to 1/200 and increased the light intensity to show her reflection on the puddle, and I increased the contrast ratio to show the reflected sky on the puddle which provides a sense of freedom to Krystal.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Americano

Americano is the considered to be Krystal’s best friend, as it allows keep her to stay awake. For this picture, I grouped two pictures together in diagonal line for viewers to better identify the difference between the two. I adjusted the shutter speed without changing the position, in order to capture two different moments while she was shaking the coffee to have a better mix. The blurring scene represents the dizziness she feels without enough sleep, and that turns out to be the reason for her to acquire the external help from coffee. The second image is very sharp and clear, which displays the effect of coffee as it stabilizes her dizziness, and Krystal is now able to continue her daily tasks. Though Americano is bitter, she has to swallow it in order to achieve high efficiency in her studies.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Phones

Cell phone has become an inseparable part of our daily lives. We use it for communication, internet surfing, catching up the newest event in the world, etc. In this photo, I edited the most commonly used apps to fill in the space on the phone screen, and they are great platforms for Krystal to share ideas and pictures online. Krystal has made lots of friends through applications such as Facebook and Weibo, as they allow her to connect people from different places around the globe.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Jogging

Body shape and appearance are emerging topics around high school students, as we start to learn how to dress and how to put on makeups. We are more sensitive to the other’s comments, and because of that, Krystal always takes out some time to jog at night to maintain a good body shape, even after a day of busy work. For this photo, I adjusted the shutter speed to 1/200 in order to capture the sweat droplets hanging on her chin, and then I took another photo of her running on the track along the riverside with higher light intensity and contrast ratio. I put these two photos together to show that caring too much about others’ opinions could cause great mental pain, just like Krystal, who can only bear the pain and exhaustion by herself.
Photo by: Sony A6400

Emotions

This image is consisted of 5 different images, and as the light intensity gradually decreases from the first one of the left to the last one, the emotion of Krystal changes drastically. Emotional instability is something that bothers high school students, which could be caused by puberty. In the morning, Krystal feels delighted because she is about to be in the school with other classmates, which is clearly demonstrated by the smile of Krystal as she rides on the bus and walks on the streets. Throughout the day, pressure accumulates from both homework and the uncertainty of the future, therefore, she experiences a sense of loneliness and melancholy, which I used dark filter to signify that emotion. I also used mosaics to blur the side between images to display that vague transition from each emotion.
Photo by: Sony A6400
